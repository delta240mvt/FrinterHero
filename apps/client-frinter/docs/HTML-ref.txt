<!-- ============================================================ SOLUTION -->
<section class="solution">
  <div class="container">
    <span class="section-label">The Solution</span>
    <h2>What if sitting down to work<br>actually meant working?</h2>

    <p class="transition-pull">What if sitting down to do focused work meant actually staying there — the phone not even entering the equation, deep reading feeling like the natural default, real work feeling rewarding again instead of like swimming upstream against your own nervous system?</p>

    <p class="sol-intro">frinter is a Focus OS. It gives you four things willpower never could: <strong>data, structure, feedback, and visibility.</strong> Here's how it works.</p>

    <div class="fcard">
      <div class="fcard-num">01 — The Core Tool</div>
      <h3>The Frint — Your Deep Work Timer</h3>
      <p class="fdoes">A "Frint" is a timed block of pure creation work. You start one — phone goes away, only real output counts. Coding, writing, strategy. Zero admin. After it ends, you rate how deep your focus actually was. That score gets recorded.</p>
      <p class="fbenefit">→ So you can see in hard numbers if you actually worked — or just sat near a laptop for 90 minutes.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="fcard">
      <div class="fcard-num">02 — Your Battery Reading</div>
      <h3>Your Energy Bar</h3>
      <p class="fdoes">An algorithm reads your sleep data and converts it into a single battery percentage each morning. Today you're at 78%. Or 29%. Now you know whether to schedule deep creative work — or book calls and easy tasks instead.</p>
      <p class="fbenefit">→ So you stop forcing 3-hour deep work sessions on 4 hours of sleep and blaming yourself when they fail.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="fcard">
      <div class="fcard-num">03 — Your Weekly Blind Spots</div>
      <h3>The FRINT Check-in</h3>
      <p class="fdoes">Once a week. 5 minutes. You score 5 areas of your life on a 1–10 scale: Flow, Relationships, Inner Balance, Nourishment, and Transcendence. It's a structured audit — not journaling, not therapy. Just five numbers you can act on.</p>
      <p class="fbenefit">→ So you catch slow burnout before it wipes out 3 productive weeks without warning.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="fcard">
      <div class="fcard-num">04 — Your Real Pattern</div>
      <h3>The Tracker</h3>
      <p class="fdoes">A monthly visual map of every focus session you've logged. You see which weeks held and which ones collapsed. You see the actual numbers — not your memory of them. No more "I think I worked hard this month."</p>
      <p class="fbenefit">→ So you stop lying to yourself about how much real work you actually did.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
